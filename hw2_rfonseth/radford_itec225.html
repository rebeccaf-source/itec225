Department of Computer and Information Sciences

The Radford Department of Computer and Information Sciences in the Artic College of Science and Technology comprises of several degree programs, including:

  • Networking
  • Cybersecurity
  • Database
  • Software Engineering
  • Computer Science

Through these various programs, this department provides a variety of noncredit and credit-bearing programs online and on campus. The Department of Computer and Information Sciences is part of Radford University and is responsible for extending excellence to students at every stage of learning.

Radford Graduate program

Study part time at Radford, in evening or online courses. Earn a degree, professional certificates, or take a course for enrichment.

At Radford Graduate School, we offer over 300 courses to help you gain new perspectives, develop a professional skill, or earn a Radford credential. Whatever your learning goals, Radford Graduate School can help you achieve them.

Radford Summer School

The Radford Summer School makes Radford's academic resources -- a distinguished faculty, well-equipped library, and a world-class university laboratory -- available to men and women of many ages, backgrounds, and nationalities through an intensive summer session of liberal arts courses on campus or online.

Our more than 300 summer courses over a wide range of subjects, including those in science, humanities, writing, economics, computer science, and many foreign languages. Some courses prepare you for advances studies in nursing and education. Others introduce you to topics like drama, dance, management, and finance.

Learning in retirment

The Radford Undergraduate program is a vibrant community of retired and semi-retired academics and professionals participating in classes and activities at Radford University.
